📚 Travel Tips

Everything you need to know before visiting Nepal — visa, weather, budget, packing and more.

Visa & Entry Requirements

Most nationalities can get a visa on arrival at Kathmandu airport

Nepal Visa on Arrival – 2025
Duration Fee (USD) Extensions Notes
15 days $30 Yes – at Immigration Office Single entry
30 days $50 Yes – up to 150 days/year Most popular option
90 days $125 Yes Best for long stays
Free visa countries: India, China (special arrangements apply)
Tip: Bring passport photos and USD cash for the visa fee — card machines may not always work at the airport.

🌅 Best Time to Visit

Nepal's weather varies dramatically by season and altitude

Season Months Weather Best For Crowds
🍁 Autumn Sept – Nov Clear skies, cool & dry Trekking, Festivals (Dashain, Tihar) High
❄️ Winter Dec – Feb Cold, snow at altitude Kathmandu, Chitwan, Pokhara Low
🍒 Spring March – May Warm, rhododendrons bloom Trekking, Climbing, Holi Medium-High
⛈️ Monsoon June – Aug Heavy rain, lush green Budget travel, Cultural visits Low (Good deals!)
Recommended: October is the single best month — perfect weather, clear mountain views, and Dashain festival!

🎒 Packing Essentials

What to bring on your Nepal adventure

🏔 For Trekking

Good trekking boots, warm layers (temperatures drop fast at altitude), a rain jacket, trekking poles, a headlamp, sunscreen SPF 50+, a sleeping bag liner, and a reusable water bottle with a filter.

🏢 For City & Culture

Modest clothing (cover shoulders and knees at temples), comfortable walking shoes, a light scarf, a small daypack, and a phrasebook or translation app. A small gift for local families you stay with is always appreciated.

💊 Health & Safety

Travel insurance is essential. Bring altitude sickness medication (Diamox — consult your doctor), water purification tablets, a basic first aid kit, insect repellent (for the lowlands), and any prescription medications.
