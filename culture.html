🏢 Culture & Festivals

Nepal is a tapestry of ancient traditions, colourful festivals, diverse religions, and warm-hearted people.

A Culture Like No Other

Home to 80+ ethnic groups, Nepal's culture is rich, diverse and deeply spiritual

⛫️ Religion & Spirituality

Nepal is the birthplace of Lord Buddha (born in Lumbini, 563 BCE) and is also home to some of Hinduism's most sacred sites including the Pashupatinath Temple — one of the holiest Shiva temples in the world.

Around 81% of Nepalis are Hindu and 9% are Buddhist. The two religions coexist beautifully here — it's common to see Hindus and Buddhists praying at the same shrines. This religious harmony is at the heart of Nepali culture.

Everywhere you go in Nepal, you'll find prayer flags fluttering in the breeze, butter lamps burning in temples, and the sound of conch shells and bells filling the air.

🏘️ The Newari Civilization

The Newar people of the Kathmandu Valley are the original inhabitants of the valley and are credited with creating much of Nepal's incredible art, architecture, and cultural traditions.

Newari cuisine is legendary — try Newari dishes like Yomari (sweet rice flour dumplings), Chatamari (rice flour crepe), bara (lentil pancakes), and Kwati (a mixed bean soup).

The Newars also built the iconic pagoda-style temples that are now found across Asia. Many historians believe the pagoda style of architecture originated in Nepal.

🎉 Major Festivals

Nepal celebrates over 50 major festivals every year — there's always something happening!

Dashain

Dashain, the longest and most significant festival in Nepal, is a spectacular celebration that showcases the country’s rich cultural heritage. Usually held in September or October, this festival marks the victory of good over evil and brings families together in joyous reunion. Visitors to Nepal during Dashain can witness vibrant rituals, traditional music and dances, and the thrilling swing festivals called ping that fill villages with laughter and excitement. Homes are adorned with flowers, red tika and jamara (barley grass), and bustling markets offer colorful handicrafts and festive foods. Experiencing Dashain provides travelers with a unique glimpse into Nepalese traditions, community spirit, and the warmth of local hospitality.

Tihar (Festival of Lights)

Tihar, also known as the Festival of Lights, is one of Nepal’s most enchanting celebrations. Spanning five days in October or November, it honors not only gods and goddesses but also animals like crows, dogs, and cows, highlighting the deep respect Nepalese culture has for all life. During Tihar, homes and streets glow with thousands of oil lamps and colorful lights, creating a magical atmosphere. Visitors can enjoy traditional music, dance, delicious festive foods, and the cheerful exchange of gifts and blessings. Experiencing Tihar offers travelers a beautiful glimpse into Nepal’s vibrant traditions, spirituality, and warm community spirit.

Holi (Festival of Colours)

Holi, the festival of colors, is one of Nepal’s most joyful and vibrant celebrations. Usually held in March, it marks the arrival of spring and the triumph of good over evil. Streets and public squares come alive as people playfully throw colored powders and water at each other, accompanied by music, singing, and dancing. Traditional sweets and drinks add to the festive spirit, while local communities gather in friendship and fun. Experiencing Holi in Nepal offers travelers a unique chance to join in the exuberance, witness Nepalese cultural traditions, and celebrate life with laughter, color, and energy.

chhath festival

Chhath is a major Hindu festival celebrated in Nepal, especially in the Terai region, to honor Surya (the Sun God) and Chhathi Maiya. It usually occurs six days after Diwali in the month of Kartika. The festival lasts four days and involves ritual fasting, holy baths, and offering prayers to the setting and rising sun for health, prosperity, and family well-being. Devotees prepare special offerings like fruits, kheer, and sugarcane, standing in rivers or ponds to perform arghya. Chhath emphasizes gratitude, purity, and devotion, connecting people closely with nature.
