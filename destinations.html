🏔 Top Destinations

From the world's highest peaks to lush jungle wildlife reserves — Nepal's destinations are truly one of a kind.

🏔 Everest Region – Solukhumbu

The Everest region is Nepal's most iconic trekking destination. Home to Mount Everest (8,849m) — the highest point on Earth — the area draws adventurers from every corner of the globe.

The classic Everest Base Camp (EBC) trek takes you through the legendary Sherpa villages of Namche Bazaar and Tengboche, offering jaw-dropping views of Everest, Lhotse, Ama Dablam and other Himalayan giants.

Best time to visit: March–May and September–November
Nearest airport: Lukla (Tenzing-Hillary Airport)

🌊 Pokhara – The Gateway to Annapurna

Nestled beside the shimmering Phewa Lake with the snow-capped Annapurna range as its backdrop, Pokhara is Nepal's most scenic city. It's a haven for backpackers, adventure seekers, and peace lovers alike.

Enjoy paragliding over the valley, boating on Phewa Lake at sunrise, or visiting the stunning Devi's Falls and the Gupteshwor Cave nearby.

Best time to visit: October–April
Distance from Kathmandu: ~200 km (6–7 hours by road)

🌿 Chitwan National Park

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Chitwan National Park is Nepal's premier wildlife destination. Located in the Terai lowlands, this lush jungle is home to Bengal tigers, one-horned rhinoceroses, elephants, leopards and over 500 bird species.

Explore the park by jeep safari, dugout canoe, or on foot with a trained naturalist guide. The thrill of spotting a rhino at close range is unforgettable.

Best time to visit: October–March
Main gateway: Sauraha village

Book Safari

🏛 Kathmandu Valley

Nepal's capital Kathmandu is a living museum of art and architecture. The valley contains seven UNESCO World Heritage Sites including Pashupatinath Temple, Boudhanath Stupa, Swayambhunath (the Monkey Temple), and the ancient Durbar Squares of Kathmandu, Patan, and Bhaktapur.

Get lost in the narrow alleys of Thamel, browse local handicraft shops, taste traditional Newari cuisine, and experience the unique spiritual atmosphere of this ancient city.

Elevation: 1,400 m
Nearest airport: Tribhuvan International Airport (TIA)
